# Futures Subdued Ahead of Fed Officials' Comments, Zscaler Falls

## Professional English
U.S. stock futures were subdued on Monday as investors awaited comments from Federal Reserve officials for clues on the central bank's monetary policy outlook. The market is looking for any hints about the timing of the tapering of the Fed's bond-buying program. Meanwhile, shares of Zscaler Inc fell after the cybersecurity company reported disappointing quarterly results.

Investors are closely watching the Federal Reserve's messaging this week as they seek clarity on the central bank's plans for tapering its massive bond-buying program. The market is eager to know when the Fed will start scaling back its monthly asset purchases, which have been supporting the economy during the pandemic. In addition, Zscaler Inc, a leading provider of cloud-based security solutions, saw its shares drop after the company reported lower-than-expected quarterly earnings. The disappointing results raised concerns about the company's growth prospects and led to a decline in its stock price.

## Simplified English
U.S. stock futures were not very active on Monday as investors waited for comments from Federal Reserve officials to get an idea of what the central bank might do with its monetary policy. People are particularly interested in finding out when the Fed will start reducing the amount of money it spends on buying bonds. At the same time, the stock price of Zscaler Inc, a company that helps protect computers and networks from cyber threats, went down after it reported its financial results for the last quarter, which were not as good as expected.

Investors are paying close attention to what the Federal Reserve says this week because they want to understand what the central bank plans to do with its big bond-buying program. They want to know when the Fed will start spending less money each month on buying bonds, which has been helping the economy during the pandemic. Also, Zscaler Inc, a company that provides security services for computers and networks using the internet, saw its stock price go down after it reported that it made less money than people thought it would in the last three months. This made investors worry that the company might not grow as much as they had hoped, and so they sold their shares, causing the price to go down.
