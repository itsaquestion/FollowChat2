\hypertarget{professional-english}{%
\section{Professional English}\label{professional-english}}

China's troubled property giant, Evergrande, is making a last-ditch
effort to avoid liquidation by proposing a debt restructuring plan. The
company, which is facing a massive debt crisis, has been struggling to
repay its creditors and has been on the brink of collapse. According to
sources, Evergrande is seeking to extend the maturity of its debt and
reduce interest payments in order to ease its financial burden. The
proposed plan is currently being reviewed by the company's creditors,
and its success will determine the fate of Evergrande and its thousands
of employees.

\section{Simplified English}\label{simplified-english}}

to repay the money it owes to its lenders and is on the verge of
collapse. According to sources, Evergrande is trying to extend the time
it has to repay its debt and reduce the amount of interest it has to pay
in order to make its financial situation more manageable. The proposed
plan is currently being reviewed by the company's lenders, and its
success will determine what happens to Evergrande and the thousands of
people who work for the company.

\chapter{Wall St.~Futures Edge Higher on Easing Inflation
Hopes}\label{wall-st.-futures-edge-higher-on-easing-inflation-hopes}}

\hypertarget{professional-english-1}{%
\section{Professional English}\label{professional-english-1}}

Wall Street futures inched higher on Tuesday as investors held onto
hopes of easing inflation. The positive sentiment was driven by
expectations that central banks would continue to support the economy
amid signs of slowing growth. Investors are closely watching the Federal
Reserve's meeting this week for any hints on its monetary policy stance.
Meanwhile, concerns over the Omicron variant of COVID-19 continue to
weigh on market sentiment, with fears of potential disruptions to global
economic recovery. Despite these uncertainties, market participants
remain cautiously optimistic, with major indices hovering near record
highs.

\hypertarget{simplified-english-1}{%
\section{Simplified English}\label{simplified-english-1}}

Wall Street futures slightly increased on Tuesday as investors remained
hopeful that inflation would ease. This positive feeling was influenced
by the belief that central banks would continue to help the economy,
especially as there are signs that growth is slowing down. Investors are
paying close attention to the Federal Reserve's meeting this week to see
if there are any indications about its plans for managing the economy.
At the same time, worries about the Omicron variant of COVID-19 are
affecting how people feel about the market. There are concerns that this
new variant could cause problems for the global economic recovery.
Despite these uncertainties, people who participate in the market are
being careful but still feel positive. The major stock market indicators
are staying close to their highest levels ever.

they say anything about what they plan to do with the economy. At the
same time, people are worried about the Omicron variant of COVID-19 and
how it might affect the market. They're afraid it could cause problems

that aren't certain, people who invest in the market are being careful
