# Futures edge up on Fed pivot hopes, jobs data in focus

## Professional English
U.S. stock index futures inched higher on Monday as investors remained optimistic about the Federal Reserve's dovish stance and awaited key jobs data. The futures pointed to a positive start for Wall Street, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq set to open near record highs. Investors are closely watching the labor market report, which is expected to show a rebound in job growth after a disappointing reading in November. The data will provide further insights into the health of the U.S. economy and could influence the Fed's decision on tapering its bond-buying program. Additionally, investors are hopeful that the central bank will maintain its accommodative monetary policy amid concerns about the Omicron variant's impact on the global economy.

The market sentiment has been buoyed by the Fed's recent comments, indicating a shift towards a more patient approach to tightening monetary policy. The central bank has signaled that it will take into account the labor market conditions and inflation outlook before raising interest rates. This has reassured investors who were concerned about the potential for a faster pace of rate hikes. The upcoming jobs data will be closely scrutinized for any signs of improvement in the employment situation, as a strong labor market is seen as a key driver of economic growth.
