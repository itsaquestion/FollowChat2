Jerome Powell Outlines Cautious Approach to Raising Rates or Declaring End to Hikes

## Professional English
Federal Reserve Chairman Jerome Powell has outlined a cautious approach to raising interest rates or declaring an end to rate hikes, signaling that the central bank will prioritize economic stability over aggressive monetary tightening. In a speech at the Economic Club of Washington, Powell emphasized the need for patience and flexibility in monetary policy, stating that the Fed will be data-dependent and will adjust its stance accordingly. He acknowledged the recent volatility in financial markets and the potential risks posed by global economic slowdown and trade tensions. Powell's remarks suggest that the Fed is taking a more cautious stance in response to the changing economic landscape, which could impact future rate hike decisions.

## Simplified English
Jerome Powell, the head of the Federal Reserve, has explained that the central bank will be careful when it comes to raising interest rates or deciding to stop increasing them. This means that the Fed will prioritize keeping the economy stable rather than aggressively tightening monetary policy. Powell made these comments during a speech at the Economic Club of Washington. He stressed the importance of being patient and flexible in making decisions about monetary policy. Powell also mentioned that the Fed will rely on data and adjust its approach accordingly. He acknowledged that financial markets have been unstable recently and that there are potential risks due to a global economic slowdown and trade tensions. Powell's remarks indicate that the Fed is taking a more cautious approach in response to the changing economic situation, which could affect future decisions about raising interest rates.
