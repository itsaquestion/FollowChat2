# Virgin Atlantic's Low-Carbon Fuel Powers Maiden Transatlantic Flight

## Professional English
Virgin Atlantic successfully completed its first transatlantic flight powered by low-carbon fuel on November 28, 2023. The flight, which traveled from London to New York, marked a significant milestone in the airline's commitment to reducing its carbon emissions. The low-carbon fuel used in the flight is made from waste gases captured from industrial steel production, and it has the potential to reduce carbon emissions by up to 75% compared to conventional jet fuel. Virgin Atlantic aims to use this sustainable fuel on all flights by 2025, as part of its efforts to achieve net-zero carbon emissions by 2050. This achievement highlights the airline's dedication to sustainability and sets an example for the aviation industry to adopt more environmentally friendly practices.
